Academic pipeline figure for a NeurIPS paper on LSME (Latent-Steered Masked Editing), a method that brings SDEdit-style controllable editing to discrete masked diffusion language models.

LSME works in three steps. Given source text (e.g., "the food was bad") and a desired target attribute (e.g., positive sentiment):

Step 1 — Partial Masking: A fraction r of source tokens are randomly replaced with [MASK] tokens. Unmasked tokens (e.g., "the", "food") are preserved exactly throughout the process via a copy flag mechanism. This creates a partially corrupted sequence like "the food [M] [M]".

Step 2 — Latent Replacement: A target latent vector z_target is computed as the centroid of training latents with the desired attribute. This clean latent (t_z = 0) replaces the source latent, acting as a conditioning signal that steers generation toward the target attribute.

Step 3 — Joint Reverse Diffusion via MMDiT: The partially masked text and the target latent are fed into a Multimodal Diffusion Transformer (MMDiT), which performs joint QKV attention between the discrete text stream and the continuous latent stream. The model denoises only the masked positions from t_entry down to t = 0, while the copy flag ensures unmasked positions remain unchanged. The result is edited text (e.g., "the food is great") where content-bearing tokens are preserved and attribute-relevant tokens are regenerated under latent guidance.
